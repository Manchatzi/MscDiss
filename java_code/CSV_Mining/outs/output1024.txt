[Engraving of the Palace of Holyroodhouse by W. Miller after C. Stanfield]  Depicts the Palace of Holyroodhouse, Edinburgh, with the Salisbury Crags and the Firth of Forth in the background. In Sir Walter Scott's Waverley (1814), the Royal Palace of Holyroodhouse is occupied by Prince Charles Edward Stuart and is the scene of a ball (ch. 43). The Palace is also described in Provincial Antiquities & Picturesque Scenery of Scotland (1826). The original steel engraving was made for the Abbotsford Edition of the Waverley Novels (1842-1847).
