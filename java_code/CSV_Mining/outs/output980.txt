Round Tower, Windsor Castle Engraved title page and frontispiece of Scott's Woodstock Title page of Woodstock, volume XL of a set of the Waverley Novels published by A. & C. Black, featuring Windsor Castle, Berkshire. In Sir Walter Scott's Woodstock (1826), Cromwell is stationed at Windsor while awaiting the capture of the fugitive King Charles. In chapter 8 of the novel Roger Wildrake is granted an audience at Windsor with Cromwell, during which he delivers a letter from Colonel Markham Everard. The original wood engraving, possibly after Edmund Evans, was made for the Abbotsford Edition of the Waverley Novels (1842-1847). Scott, W. Woodstock. Edinburgh: Adam and Charles Black, [185-?]   Windsor Castle
