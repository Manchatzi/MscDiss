Murder of the Regent Murray in Linlithgow Steel engraving by Charles Heath from a drawing by G. Cattermole of a scene from the poem Cadyow Castle Depicts James Hamilton of Bothwellhaugh (d. 1581), a supporter of Mary Queen of Scots, preparing to assassinate James Stewart, 1st Earl of Moray (ca. 1531-1570), as depicted in Sir Walter Scott's ballad 'Cadyow Castle'. The ballad-imitation Cadyow Castle was first published in The Minstrelsy of the Scottish Borders, vol. III (1803). Ritchie, L. Scott and Scotland. London: Longman & Co., 1835 (facing p. 221)
