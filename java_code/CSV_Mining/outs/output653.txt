Hawthornden Engraving of Hawthornden Castle by an unattributed artist Depicts the North Esk River and Hawthornden Castle, Midlothian. The glen of Hawthornden, near Roslin, Midlothian, features in a number of Sir Walter Scott's works, including Rokeby, The Antiquary, 'The Gray Brother', and, in particular, The Lay of the Last Minstrel where 'a wondrous blaze... | 'Twas seen from Dryden's groves of oak, | And seen from cavern'd Hawthornden.' (canto VI, stanza XXIII, lines 26-32). The site is also described in Scott's Provincial Antiquities & Picturesque Scenery of Scotland (1826). The castle itself was built by the poet William Drummond in the seventeenth century. Chambers, R. The Picture of Scotland. Vol. II. Edinburgh: W.
