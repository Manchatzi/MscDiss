The Guard House Original pencil drawing by James Skene of the Guard House, Edinburgh Original pencil drawing by James Skene depicting the headquarters of the City Guard in Edinburgh's High Street as mentioned in chapter 5 of Scott's novel The Heart of Mid-Lothian. This drawing was subsequently etched for Skene's A Series of Sketches of the Existing Localities of the Waverley Novels (Edinburgh: Robert Cadell, 1829). It also shows the Tron Kirk and a group of buildings, including the Black Turnpike, demolished to permit the building of the South Bridge in 1786.    Edinburgh
