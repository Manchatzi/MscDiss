Inn at Howgate Engraved title page and frontispiece of Scott's Saint Ronan's Well Title page of St. Ronan's Well, volume XXXIII of a set of the Waverley Novels published by A. & C. Black, depicting the inn at Howgate, near Penicuik, Midlothian. The inn at Howgate has sometime been proposed as a model for the Auld Town Inn kept by Meg Dods in Sir Walter Scott's Saint Ronan's Well (1823). The original wood engraving, probably after Noel Paton, was made for the Abbotsford edition of the Waverley Novels (1842-1847). Scott, W. Saint Ronan's Well. Edinburgh: Adam and Charles Black, [185-?]   Howgate
