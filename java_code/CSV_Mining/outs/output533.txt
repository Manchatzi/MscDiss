Lady of the Lake Engraving by F. Engleheart from a drawing by R. Westall illustrating a scene from Scott's poem The Lady of the Lake Depicts Ellen Douglas's surprise at the sudden appearance of James Fitz-James, with the harper Allan-bane in the background (Lady of the Lake, canto IV, stanza 16). Illustrates canto IV, stanza 16, lines 7-8: 'Ellen beheld as in a dream, | Then, starting, scarce suppressed a scream'. Scott, W. The Lady of the Lake: Illustrated with Engravings from the Designs of Richd. Westall. London: John Sharpe, 1811 (facing p. 166)
