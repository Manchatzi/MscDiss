Tantallon Castle Lithograph of Tantallon Castle by T. Picken after C. Stanfield Depicts Tantallon Castle, East Lothian, on the Firth of Forth. In Canto Fifth of Marmion (1808), James IV of Scotland sends Lord Marmion and Clara de Clare to Tantallon Castle: 'And, sudden, close before them showed | His towers Tantallon vast; | Broad, massive, high, and stretching far, | And held impregnable in war. | On a projecting rock they rose, | And round three sides the ocean flows' (stanza 23, lines 4-8). Tantallon Castle, built by the Earls of Douglas in the fourteenth century, is also described in Scott's Provincial Antiquities & Picturesque Scenery of Scotland (1826). Lawson, J.P. Scotland Delineated in a Series of Views by Clarkson Stanfield. London: J. Hogarth. 1847-1854.   Tantallon Castle
