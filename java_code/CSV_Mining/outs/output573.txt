Walter Scott (1808) Engraved portrait of Sir Walter Scott by J. Horsburgh after H. Raeburn Full-length portrait facing left of Sir Walter Scott at the age of thirty-six, seated with Camp at his feet and Hermitage Castle, Liddesdale, Scottish Borders, in the background. Scott sat for the original portrait by Sir Henry Raeburn in spring 1808. Commissioned by Archibald Constable, it was completed in the same year. It is now in the possession of the Duke of Buccleuch and Queensberry at Bowhill. The engraving by Horsburgh was originally made for the 2nd edition of J.G. Lockhart's Life of Scott in 1839. Scott, W. Waverley: or 'Tis Sixty Years Since. Centenary Edition. Edinburgh: Adam and Charles Black, 1886. (front.)
