Edinburgh Engraving of a view of Edinburgh by W.H. Lizars after J.B. Kidd Presents a view of Edinburgh from Corstorphine Hill. Sir Walter Scott was born and raised in Edinburgh and worked there, first as an Advocate from 1792 to 1806, then as Clerk of the Court of Session until his retirement in 1830. The city features in many of his works, most notably The Heart of Mid-Lothian (1818). The original work by Joseph Bartholomew Kidd was probably made expressly for Robert Chambers's The Picture of Scotland, a publication aimed at the tourist market. Chambers, R. The Picture of Scotland. Vol. II. Edinburgh: William Tait, 1827 (facing p.78)   Edinburgh
