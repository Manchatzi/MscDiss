[Engraving of the Trossachs by W. Richardson after A. Nasmyth]  Depicts a view of the Trossachs, Stirling. The Trossachs region of Scotland, now a National Park, was home to Rob Roy Macgregor and his clan. Sir Walter Scott celebrated its scenery in the novel Rob Roy (1817) and poem The Lady of the Lake (1810), spawning an enduring tourist industry. Scott visited the region with his wife in the summers of 1809, 1816, and 1817. The original steel engraving was made for the Abbotsford edition of the Waverley Novels (1842-1847). Scott, W. Rob Roy.
