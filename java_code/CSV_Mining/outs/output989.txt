Berwick-upon-Tweed Engraving of Berwick-upon-Tweed by W. Miller after J.M.W. Turner Depicts Berwick-upon-Tweed, Northumberland, with the ruins of Berwick Castle in the foreground. Sir Walter Scott's drama Halidon Hill (1822) describes an event which took place during the Battle of Halidon Hill (1333), when a Scottish army under Sir Archibald Douglas sought to lift the siege of Berwick-upon-Tweed but were heavily defeated by English forces. This engraving was first published in vol. XII of the The Poetical Works of Sir Walter Scott, Bart. (Edinburgh: Cadell, 1834).    Berwick-upon-Tweed Halidon Hill, Battle of, 1333
