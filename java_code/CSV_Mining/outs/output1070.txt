John Home Engraved portrait of John Home by J. Rogers after H. Raeburn Engraved half-length portrait of Scottish dramatist John Home (1722-1808). The Scottish dramatist John Home befriended the infant Sir Walter Scott and his aunt Jenny when they were taking the waters in Bath in 1775-1776. Scott subsequently became a frequent visitor to Home's villa at Kilduff outside Edinburgh. Scott reviewed Henry Mackenzie's edition of Home's Works (1822) in the Quarterly Review for June 1827, providing a detailed account of Home's life. The original portrait by Raeburn was painted ca. 1795-1800. Chambers, R. (ed.) A Biographical Dictionary of Eminent Scotsmen, vol. III. Glasgow: Blackie, 1835.
