Blenheim Coloured wood block engraving of Blenheim Palace by B. Fawcett after A.F. Lydon Depicts a view of Blenheim Palace, Oxfordshire, including the lake and Vanbrugh's Grand Bridge. The royal park and lodge featured in Sir Walter Scott's Woodstock have since been levelled and replaced by Blenheim Palace and the gardens of Capability Brown. The Palace is also mentioned in Waverley and Kenilworth. Scott visited Blenheim Palace with Richard Heber in May 1803. The original drawing (on wood) by A.F. Lydon was made expressly for The Ancestral Homes of Britain by F.O. Morris. Morris, F.O. The Ancestral Homes of Britain. London: Bell and Daldy, 1868   Blenheim Palace
