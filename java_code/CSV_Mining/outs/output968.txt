Statue of George Heriot: (In the Hospital at Edinburgh) Engraved title page and frontispiece of Scott's The Fortunes of Nigel Title page of The Fortunes of Nigel, volume XXVI of a set of the Waverley Novels published by A. & C. Black, depicting a statue of George Heriot (1563-1624). George Heriot, Goldsmith and Jeweller to James VI, is portrayed as 'Jingling Geordie' in Sir Walter Scott's 1822 novel The Fortunes of Nigel. George Heriot's School, Edinburgh, formerly George Heriot's Hospital, was founded in 1628 as a result of a bequest from Heriot. The statue of Heriot in the school's Quadrangle was carved in 1693 by Robert Mylne (1633-1710) who based the likeness on a painting by Paul Van Somer. This engraving, obtained glyphographically be Edward Palmer, was originally made for the Abbotsford Edition of the Waverley Novels (1842-1847). Scott, W. The Fortunes of Nigel. Edinburgh: Adam and Charles Black, [185-?] Edinburgh George Heriot's School
