Nicolao Copernico: From a Picture in the Possession of the Royal Society Presented by Dr. Wolf of Danzic, June 6, 1776 Steel engraved portrait of Copernicus by E. Scriven Engraved portrait of the Polish astronomer Nicolaus Copernicus (1473-1543). In a footnote to his edition of The Works of Jonathan Swift (1814) Sir Walter Scott alludes to the tradition that Copernicus discovered that the world revolved during a drunken fit. The engraving is from a portrait by an unknown artist copied by Friedrich Anton Lohrmann (ca. 1735-after 1776). The Portrait Gallery of Distinguished Poets, vol. III. London: Wm. S. Orr & Co., 1853
