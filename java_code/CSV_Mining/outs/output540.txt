Thomas the Rhymer Engraving by F. Engleheart from a drawing by R. Westall illustrating a scene from Scott's poem Thomas the Rhymer Depicts Thomas the Rhymer bidding farewell to his home of Ercildoune (present-day Earlston, Berwickshire) (Thomas the Rhymer, part III, stanza 38). Illustrates part III, stanza 38, lines 3-4: 'And there, before Lord Douglas' face, | With them he cross'd the flood.' This modern continuation of the ancient ballad Thomas the Rhymer was first published in The Minstrelsy of the Scottish Borders, vol. II (1802). Scott, W. Glenfinlas, and Other Ballads, etc.: Illustrated with Engravings from the Designs of Richd. Westall. London: J. Sharpe, 1812 (facing p. 130)
