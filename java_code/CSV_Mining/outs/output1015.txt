Convent of St. Saba, Wilderness of Engadi, Holy Land Engraving of the Convent of St Saba by J. Horsburgh after D. Roberts Depicts the monastery of Mar Saba overlooking the Kidron Valley (West Bank). In chapter 6 of Sir Walter Scott's The Talisman (1825), King Richard of England is informed that his wife Queen Berengaria has set out on a pilgrimage to the 'Convent of Engadi', i.e. the Greek-Orthodox Monastery Mar Saba. The original steel engraving was made for the Abbotsford Edition of the Waverley Novels (1842-1847). Scott, W. The Talisman. Edinburgh: Adam and Charles Black, [185-?]   Mar Saba (Monastery)
