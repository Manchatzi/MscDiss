Ben Lawers, From the South Shore of Loch Tay Engraving of Ben Lawers by R. Brandard after C. Stanfield Depicts the south face of Ben Lawers as seen from Loch Tay, Perthshire. In ch. 27 of Sir Walter Scott's The Fair Maid of Perth (1828), the mountains seen from the north bank of Loch Tay are likened to a 'clan of Titans [...] commanded by their appropriate chieftains -- the frowning mountain of Ben Lawers, and the still more lofty eminence of Ben Mohr'. The original steel engraving was made for the Abbotsford Edition of the Waverley Novels (1842-1847). Scott, W. The Fair Maid of Perth. Edinburgh: Adam and Charles Black, [185-?]   Ben Lawers
