Leibnitz: From a Picture in the Florence Gallery Engraved portrait of Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz by B. Holl after an unknown artist Engraved portrait of the German philosopher and mathematician, Gottfried Wilhelm Leibnitz (1646-1716). While working on his 1829 novel Anne of Geierstein, Sir Walter Scott asked Robert Cadell to procure a number of works dealing with the Vehmgericht (Invisible Tribunal) in Germany. In a letter of 6 August 1828 he requested a copy of the Scriptores Rerum Brunswicensium, edited by the philosopher and mathematician Leibnitz. The Portrait Gallery of Distinguished Poets. Vol. II. London: Wm. S. Orr & Co, 1853
