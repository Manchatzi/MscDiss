[Title page of James Hogg's The Brownie of Bodsbeck; and Other Tales]  James Hogg's novel The Brownie of Bodsbeck is a fictional account of the 17th-century Covenanting movement, written in response to Scott's Old Mortality, and offering a more sympathetic portrayal of the Covenanters. Scott met the poet and novelist James Hogg while researching material for Minstrelsy of the Scottish Border (1802-03). Scott remained his lifelong friend and patron. Following Scott's death, Hogg published The Domestic Manners and Private Life of Sir Walter Scott (1834). Hogg, J. The Brownie of Bodsbeck. Edinburgh: William Blackwood, 1818
