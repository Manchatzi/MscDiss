Greta Hall and Keswick Bridge Engraving of Keswick Bridge and Greta Hall by E. Francis after W. Westall Depicts Keswick Bridge, Cumbria, with Greta Hall in the background. Greta Hall, when the home of Samuel Taylor Coleridge and, later, Robert Southey, played host to many literary figures including Sir Walter Scott, who visited Southey in August 1805, August 1813 and August 1825. In 1813, Scott was offered the post of Poet Laureate, but refused and instead suggested Southey. Moule, T. The Landscape Album, or, Great Britain Illustrated: In a Series of Sixty Views. London: Charles Tilt, 1832   Greta Hall
