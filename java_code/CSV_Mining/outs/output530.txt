Lady of the Lake Engraving by C. Heath from a drawing by R. Westall of the heroine of Scott's poem The Lady of the Lake Depicts Ellen Douglas hearing James Fitz-James's hunting horn (Lady of the Lake, canto I, stanza 17). Illustrates canto I, stanza 17, lines 23-24: 'In listening mood, she seemed to stand, | The guardian Naiad of the strand.' Scott, W. The Lady of the Lake: Illustrated with Engravings from the Designs of Richd. Westall. London: John Sharpe, 1811 (facing p. 22)
