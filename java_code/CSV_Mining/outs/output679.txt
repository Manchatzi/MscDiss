Loch Katrine & Helen's Isle (Perthshire): Looking South Engraving of Loch Katrine and Ellen's Isle by J. Swan after J. Fleming Depicts Ellen's Isle in Loch Katrine, Stirling. In Sir Walter Scott's The Lady of the Lake (1810), the Earl of Bothwell and his daughter Ellen take refuge on an island in Loch Katrine. Following the success of the poem, the Loch became a major tourist attractions and one of its islands was identified as Scott's setting and became known as 'Ellen's Isle'. Leighton, J.M.
