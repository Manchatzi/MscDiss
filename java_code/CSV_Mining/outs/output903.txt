[Theatre Royal, Edinburgh playbill for The Heart of Midlothian, a musical drama adopted from Sir Walter Scott's novel]  Playbill for The Heart of Midlothian, also advertising Thirty Years of a Gambler's Life, among other productions. The 'national drama' The Heart of Midlothian is probably a version of The Heart of Midlothian, or, The Lily of St. Leonards, a musical drama adapted from Sir Walter Scott's 1818 novel with words by Thomas John Dibdin (1771-1841) and music by Erskine. It was first performed at the Royal Circus, London, on 13 January 1819.
