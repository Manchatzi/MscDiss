Isabel Vere Steel engraving by J. Thomson from a drawing by E. T. Paris of a character from Walter Scott's novel The Black Dwarf Depicts Isabel Vere fearfullly knocking at the door of the Black Dwarf's hut (Black Dwarf, ch. 16). The portrait illustrates the words: 'She twice extended her hand to the door, and twice she withdrew it; and when she did at length make the effort, the knock did not equal in violence the throb of her own bosom.' Portraits of the Principal Female Characters in the Waverley Novels. London: Charles Tilt, 1834 (p. 7)
