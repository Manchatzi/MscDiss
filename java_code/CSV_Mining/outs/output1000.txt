Barnbugle Castle and Firth of Forth: From Laurieston Castle. Engraving of the Barnbougle Castle and the Firth of Forth by W. Miller after C. Stanfield Depicts the Firth of Forth from Lauriston Castle, Edinburgh, with cattle in the foreground. In Sir Walter Scott's The Abbot (1820), the hero Roland Graeme 'admired the Gothic towers of Barnbougle, rising from the seabeaten rock, and overlooking one of the most glorious landscapes in Scotland' (ch. 20). The thirteenth-century tower house is the seat of the Earls of Roseberry. The original steel engraving was made for the Abbotsford edition of the Waverley Novels (1842-1847). Scott, W. The Abbot. Edinburgh: Adam and Charles Black, [185-?]   Barnbougle Castle
