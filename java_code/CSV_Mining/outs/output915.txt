Roslin Castle Lithograph of Rosslyn Castle by T. Picken after J. D. Harding Depicts the ruins of Rosslyn Castle, Midlothian. Rosslyn (or Roslin) Castle is featured in Sir Walter Scott's Lay of the Last Minstrel (1805) in the ballad of 'Rosabelle' recited by the bard Harold: 'O'er Roslin all that dreary night, | A wondrous blaze was seen to gleam; | 'Twas broader than the watch-fire's light, | And redder than the bright moon-beam' (canto VI, stanza XXIII, lines 25-28). The castle, a ruined 14th-century building located 9 miles south of Edinburgh, is also described in Scott's Provincial Antiquities & Picturesque Scenery of Scotland (1826). Lawson, J.P.
