Rigaud Engraved portrait of the French divine and historian Jacques-B̩nigne Bossuet (1627-1704). In his Life of John Dryden (1808) Scott discusses the role played by the theological writings of Bossuet in Dryden's conversion to Catholicism. In an article on Moli̬re for the Foreign Quarterly Review in 1828, he also defends the playwright against Bossuet's charge of profanity. The original portrait by Hyacinthe Rigaud was painted in 1702. The Portrait Gallery of Distinguished Poets, vol. II. London: Wm. S. Orr & Co., 1853
