Salisbury Crags Near Edinburgh Engraving of Salisbury Crags by J. Fife after W. Westall Depicts the Salisbury Crags, Edinburgh, with Arthur's Seat in the background. The Salisbury Crags are described in Sir Walter Scott's Provincial Antiquities & Picturesque Scenery of Scotland (1826). According to the General Preface to the Magnum Opus Edition of the Waverley Novels (1829) Scott entertained his school-friends with his story-telling abilities during long walks in their vicinity. According to Lockhart, Scott also first tried his hand at composing verse during rambles on the Crags. Moule, T. The Landscape Album, or, Great Britain Illustrated: In a Series of Sixty Views.
