View on the Coast of Zetland: The Morning after a Storm Engraving of Shetland (Zetland) by W. Miller after W. Collins Depicts wreckage off the Shetland coast. In Sir Walter Scott's The Pirate (1822), Captain Cleveland is shipwrecked on the coast of Shetland, where most of the novel is set. Scott visited Shetland in 1814 when he joined a party of Commissioners for the Northern Lighthouse Service on a voyage of inspection around the coast of Scotland. The original steel engraving was made for the Abbotsford Edition of the Waverley Novels (1842-1847). Scott, W. The Pirate. Edinburgh: Adam and Charles Black, [185-?]   Shetland
