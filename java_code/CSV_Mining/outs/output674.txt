Loch Katrine, Perthshire Engraving of Loch Katrine by R. Sands after T. Allom Depicts a man and horse in a secluded bay of Loch Katrine, Stirling. Loch Katrine provides the setting for Sir Walter Scott's The Lady of the Lake (1810). Following the success of the poem, the Loch and the surrounding Trossachs region became major tourist attractions. The original work by Thomas Allom was made expressly for William Beattie's Scotland Illustrated (1836). The Complete Works of Robert Burns and the Poetical Works of Sir Walter Scott. Vol. II.
