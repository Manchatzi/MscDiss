Edinburgh: From near Craigmillar Castle Lithograph of Edinburgh and Craigmillar Castle by T. Picken after W. Simpson Depicts Craigmillar Castle with Edinburgh in the distance. Craigmillar Castle, built in the 15th century by the Preston family, was a favourite residence of Mary Queen of Scots. In ch. 17 of Sir Walter Scott's The Abbot (1820), when Roland Graeme catches a first glimpse of Edinburgh, his companion Adam Woodcock points out Craigmillar Castle 'which I have known a merry place in my time'. The castle is also mentioned in Scott's account of Mary's reign in Tales of a Grandfather, First Series (1828). It is also described in detail in his Provincial Antiquities & Picturesque Scenery of Scotland (1826). Lawson, J.P. Scotland Delineated: In a Series of Views by Clarkson Stanfield. London: J.
