Mr. Mackay of the Theatre Royal Edinburgh as Baillie Nicol Jarvie Title page and frontispiece engraving by E. Mitchell of a scene from a dramatization of Scott's novel Rob Roy Depicts the actor Charles Mackay (d. 1857) as Baillie Nicol Jarvie in Isaac Pocock's stage adaptation of Sir Walter Scott's novel Rob Roy. The print was engraved by Mitchell from his own design. Pocock's Rob Roy Macgregor, or, Auld Lang Syne, with music by Sir Henry Bishop (1786-1855) and John Davy (1763-1824), was first performed at Covent Garden, London, on 12 March 1818 and went on to become one of the most popular and frequently revived dramas of the nineteenth century. Dramas from the Novels and Tales of the Author of 'Waverley', no. 1. Edinburgh: James L. Huie, 1822
