Dunstanborough Castle, Northumberland Engraving of Dunstanburgh Castle by W. Floyd after J.W. Carmichael Depicts a storm-tossed sea boat with Dunstanburgh Castle, near Alnwick, Northumberland, in the background. Dunstanburgh (or Dunstanborough) Castle, dominates a stretch of coastline portrayed in Scott's Marmion: 'And next, they crossed themselves, to hear | The whitening breakers sound so near, | Where, boiling through the rocks, they roar | On Dunstanborough's caverned shore' (canto II, stanza 7, lines 17-20). The Gallery of Modern British Artists. London: Simpkin and Marshall, 1834. (facing p.42)   Dunstanburgh Castle
