Brown Square: From the Society Engraving of Brown Square, Edinburgh by W. Forrest after D. Wilson Depicts a scene of daily life in Brown Square, Edinburgh. Brown Square or Brown's Square, named after the architect James Brown, was built as a residential development in 1763. It is the fictional home of Alan Fairford's family in Scott's novel Redgauntlet (1824). Brown Square was demolished in the 19th century to permit the building of George IV Bridge and Chambers Street. The original painting by Sir Daniel Wilson was made expressly for his book Memorials of Edinburgh in the Olden Time, which was first published in 1848. Wilson, D. Memorials of Edinburgh in the Olden Time. Vol. II. Edinburgh: A. and C. Black, 1891 (facing p.144)   Edinburgh
