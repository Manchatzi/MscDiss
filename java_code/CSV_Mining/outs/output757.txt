Author of 'The Miscellany' Facsimile reproduction of a print portrait of Sir John Cam Hobhouse by Daniel Maclise Sketch of British statesman John Cam Hobhouse (1786-1869) addressing an election crowd, with facsimile of signature. John Cam Hobhouse, Lord Broughton, was one of Lord Byron's closest friends. Such was his loyalty to the poet that he came to be known as 'Byron's bulldog'. Hobhouse corresponded with Scott in 1826 and 1828 concerning a monument to Byron to which Scott subscribed. This facsimile reproduction is of one of a series of portraits originally published in Fraser's Magazine, 1830-38, under the title of 'A Gallery of Illustrious Literary Characters'. Maclise, D. The Maclise Portrait Gallery of Illustrious Literary Characters, ed. W. Bates. London: Chatto and Windus, 1883 (facing p.190)
