Pinkie House Drawing of Pinkie House by W. Geikie Depicts Pinkie House, Musselburgh, East Lothian. In Sir Walter Scott's novel Waverley (1814), ch. 49-50, Charles Edward Stuart ('Bonnie Prince Charlie') establishes his headquarters at Pinkie House near Musselburgh following his victory at the Battle of Prestonpans. Built as a tower house in the sixteenth century, Pinkie House was greatly expanded by Sir Alexander Seton, Chancellor of Scotland, in 1613.
