Inverary Castle, & Dun-y-quaich, Argyll-shire Engraving of Inveraray Castle by J. Swan after J. Fleming Depicts Inveraray Castle, Argyll and Bute, and surrounding countryside. The original castle of Inveraray features prominently as the seat of the Marquis of Argyle in Sir Walter Scott's novel A Legend of Montrose (1819). It was burnt down by the Marquis of Montrose in 1644 and the present castle, depicted here, was built in the 1740s. There are further references to Inveraray as the seat of the Duke of Argyll in The Heart of Mid-Lothian. Leighton, J.M. The Lakes of Scotland: A Series of Views. Glasgow: J.
