Mr. Mackenzie Engraved portrait of Henry Mackenzie by W. H. Lizars after J. G. Lockhart Engraved portrait of Scottish novelist Henry Mackenzie (1745-1831). Scott read Henry Mackenzie's novels in his youth and became his friend and colleague in adulthood. Mackenzie contributed to Walter Scott's Edinburgh Annual Register and both men were trustees of the Edinburgh Theatre. In 1823, Scott edited Mackenzie's fiction for Ballantyne's Novelists Library, and provided a biographical sketch of the author which was subsequently reprinted as one of his Lives of the Novelists. This portrait was engraved by William Home Lizars, son of Daniel Lizars. Although Daniel Lizars died in 1812, the family occasionally continued to use W&D Lizars as their signature. The artist signature P.M. refers to Peter Morris, the alias adopted by J.G. Lockhart in Peter's Letters to his Kinfolk. Lockhart, J. G. Peter's Letters to His Kinsfolk. Vol. II. Edinburgh: William Blackwood. 1819. (facing p.99)
