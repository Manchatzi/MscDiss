The Walls of Constantinople and Distant View of the Sea of Marmara Engraving of the Walls of Constantinople by W. Forrest after T. Allom Depicts the city walls of Istanbul, Turkey. Sir Walter Scott's Count Robert of Paris is set in Constantinople during the reign of the Byzantine Emperor Alexius Comnenus (1081-1118).The opening scene takes place outside the Golden Gate before 'the massive, and seemingly impregnable walls with which Constantine surrounded the city' and which 'were greatly improved and added to by Theodosius' (ch. 2). The original steel engraving was made for the Abbotsford Edition of the Waverley Novels (1842-1847). Scott, W. Count Robert of Paris. Edinburgh: Adam and Charles Black, [185-?]   Istanbul
