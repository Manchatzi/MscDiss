Altrive: The Residence of the Ettrick Shepherd Engraving of Altrive by W. Richardson after D.O. Hill Depicts Altrieve Cottage, near Ettrick, Selkirkshire, with James Hogg and family in the foreground. Altrieve Cottage was the home of poet and novelist James Hogg, known as the 'Ettrick Shepherd'. Sir Walter Scott met Hogg while researching material for Minstrelsy of the Scottish Border and became his lifelong friend and patron. Hogg, J. Tales and Sketches by the Ettrick Shepherd. Vol. V. Glasgow: Blackie & son, 1837 (t.p.)   Altrieve Farm
