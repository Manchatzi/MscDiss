Clerihugh's Tavern, Writer's Court, Edinburgh Engraved title page and frontispiece of Scott's Guy Mannering Title page of Guy Mannering, volume IV of a set of the Waverley Novels published by A. & C. Black, depicting Writer's Court, Edinburgh. In chapter 7 of Guy Mannering (1815), Mannering and Dandie Dinmont seek the lawyer Counsellor Pleydell in Clerihugh's Tavern, Edinburgh, and find him engaged in a game of 'High Jinks'. Clerihugh's Tavern was a real-life locale where the lawyers and magistrates of Edinburgh transacted much of their business in the 18th century. The original wood engraving, probably based on the work of Joseph Noel Paton, was made for the Abbotsford edition of the Waverley Novels (1842-47). Scott, W. Guy Mannering. Edinburgh: Adam and Charles Black, [185-?]   Edinburgh
