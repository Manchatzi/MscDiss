Traquair Facsimile of a painting of Traquair House by E.W. Haslehust Depicts Traquair House, Scottish Borders, behind closed gates. Traquair House, near Peebles, parts of which date from the twelfth century, is considered to be the oldest continually inhabited house in Scotland. It was one of Walter Scott's favourite haunts and has been suggested as a model for the castles of Tully-Veolan in Waverley (1814) and Ellieslaw in The Black Dwarf (1816). Geddie, J.
