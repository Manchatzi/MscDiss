Fleshmarket Close, Edinburgh Lithograph of Fleshmarket Close, Edinburgh by T. Picken after G. Cattermole Depicts Fleshmarket Close, Edinburgh. In the Magnum Opus edition of The Heart of Mid-Lothian (1830), Scott inserts a note containing a contemporary account of the inquiries made by Crown Counsel into the lynching of Captain Porteous in Edinburgh in 1737. This describes the unsuccessful attempt to arrest a suspect, Thomas Burns, a butcher, in an alehouse in Fleshmarket Close. Fleshmarket Close was intersected by Cockburn Street in 1859. Only the lower part of the historical Close now retains that name. Lawson, J.P. Scotland Delineated: In a Series of Views by Clarkson Stanfield. London: J. Hogarth, 1847-1854   Edinburgh
