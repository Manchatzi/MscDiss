Theodosius' Column at Constantinople Engraved title page and frontispiece of Scott's Count Robert of Paris Title page of Count Robert of Paris, volume XLVI of a set of the Waverley Novels published by A. & C Black, depicting the Column of Theodosius, Istanbul. Sir Walter Scott's Count Robert of Paris (1831) is set in Constantinople during the reign of the Byzantine Emperor Alexius Comnenus (1081-1118). It begins with a brief history of the city which mentions the additions and improvements made by the Emperor Theodosius I (378-395). It was Theodosius who brought to Constantinople the Egyptian obelisk of Pharaoh Thutmosi III now known as the Column of Theodosius. The original wood engraving was made for the Abbotsford Edition of the Waverley Novels (1842-1847). Scott, W. Count Robert of Paris. Edinburgh: Adam and Charles Black, [185-?] Hippodrome of Constantinople Istanbul
