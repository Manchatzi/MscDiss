The Castle Hall Steel engraving by J. Mitchell after a painting by C. R. Leslie of a scene from Scott's poem The Lay of the Last Mistrel Depicts a child playing at soldiers and an armoured guard. John Banim (1798-1842) and his brother Michael Banim (1796-1874) wrote national tales and novels, using the pseudonyms Barnes and Abel O'Hara, with the aim of doing for Ireland what Scott had done for Scotland. This engraving may originally have been intended as an illustration for Scott's The Lay of the Last Minstrel, possibly portraying Lady Branksome's infant son. The Keepsake for MDCCCXXX, ed. Frederic Mansel Reynolds. London: Hurst, Chance & Co., 1829   Republic of Ireland
