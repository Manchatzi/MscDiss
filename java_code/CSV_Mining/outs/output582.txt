The Bridge at Ashestiel Facsimile of a pencil drawing of Ashestiel Bridge by G. Home Depicts a bridge over the River Tweed at Ashestiel, near Selkirk, Scottish Borders. Ashestiel was Sir Walter Scott's summer residence from 1804 to 1811. It was here that Marmion and the first abortive chapters of Waverley were written. When the Ashestiel lease expired in May 1811 the Scott family moved to the site which was to become Abbotsford. Baikie, J. The Charm of the Scott Country. London: A. & C.
