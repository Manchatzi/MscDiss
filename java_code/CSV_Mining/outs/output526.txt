Marmion Engraving by S. Noble from a drawing by R. Westall illustrating a scene from Scott's poem Marmion Depicts Sir Ralph de Wilton, disguised as a palmer, facing Marmion in the hostel (Marmion, canto III, stanza 5). Illustrates canto III, stanza 5, lines 5-7: 'Still fixed on Marmion was his look, | Which he, who ill such gaze could brook, | Strove by a frown to quell'. Scott, W. Marmion: Illustrated with Engravings from the Designs of Richard Westall. London: John Sharpe, 1809 (facing p. 138)    Flodden, Battle of, England, 1513
