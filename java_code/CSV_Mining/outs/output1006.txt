James 1st Engraving of James I by G. B. Shaw after the original at the Palace of Holyroodhouse Engraved portrait of King James VI and I (1566-1625) in ermine fringed robes. In Walter Scott's The Fortunes of Nigel (1822), the hero Nigel Olifaunt, Lord Glenvarloch travels to the court of King James VI and I at Whitehall, in order to reclaim a sum of money lent to the King by his father. The original steel engraving was made for the Abbotsford edition of the Waverley Novels (1842-1847). It derives from a portrait painted in 1618 by Paulus van Somer. Scott, W. The Fortunes of Nigel. Edinburgh: Adam and Charles Black, [185-?]
