Looking West up Fleet Street to the Law Courts Facsimile of a painting of Fleet Street by E.W. Haslehust Depicts a view down Fleet Street, London, to the Royal Courts of Justice. Fleet Street is the location for the shop of David Ramsay, the watchmaker, in Sir Walter Scott's The Fortunes of Nigel (1822). Nigel's retainer Richie Moniplies is wounded in an affray on Fleet Street in the first chapter of the novel. Jerrold, W. The Heart of London.
