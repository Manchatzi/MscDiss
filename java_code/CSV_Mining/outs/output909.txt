[Theatre Royal, Edinburgh playbill for Rob Roy, a musical drama adapted from Sir Walter Scott's novel]  Playbill for Rob Roy; also advertises My Neighbour's Wife and The Twa Drovers, among other productions. The 'national opera' Rob Roy is probably a version of the musical drama Rob Roy Macgregor, or, Auld Lang Syne, written by Isaac Pocock (1783-1835) with music by Sir Henry Bishop (1786-1855) and John Davy (1763-1824). Adapted from Sir Walter Scott's 1817 novel, it was first performed at Covent Garden, London, on 12 March 1818 and went on to become one of the most popular and frequently revived dramas of the nineteenth century.
