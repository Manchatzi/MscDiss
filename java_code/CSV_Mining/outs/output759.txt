The Author of 'The Chaldee Manuscript' Facsimile reproduction of a print portrait of James Hogg by Daniel Maclise Sketch of Scottish author James Hogg (1770-1835) draped in a plaid, with facsimile of signature. Sir Walter Scott met the poet and novelist James Hogg while researching material for Minstrelsy of the Scottish Border (1802-1803) and became his lifelong friend and patron. Following Scott's death, Hogg published The Domestic Manners and Private Life of Sir Walter Scott (1834). The 'Chaldee Manuscript' was a satire on Edinburgh's literary scene published in Blackwood's Magazine (1817). This facsimile reproduction is of one of a series of portraits originally published in Fraser's Magazine, 1830-1838, under the title of 'A Gallery of Illustrious Literary Characters'. The portrait is signed 'A. Croquis', an alias occasionally used by Maclise. Maclise, D. The Maclise Portrait Gallery of Illustrious Literary Characters, ed. W. Bates. London: Chatto and Windus, 1883 (facing p.54)
